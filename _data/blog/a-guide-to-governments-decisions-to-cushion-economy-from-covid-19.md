Here's a guide to the measures announced so far:

**For Banks:**

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->CHEAPER CASH: A series of steps announced this year aim to encourage banks to lend.

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Banks don't need to set aside cash reserves for loans given to small businesses between January 31 to July 31, or for credit to help consumers buy a car or home (announced February 6)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Policy lending rate -- the repurchase rate -- cut by 75 basis points in a single move this year. However, the effective deposit rate has been slashed by 115 basis points to discourage lenders from playing safe and parking the cash with the RBI (March 27 and April 17)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Cash Reserve Ratio reduced to 3 per cent from 4 per cent (March 27)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Liquidity Coverage Ratio lowered to 80 per cent from 100 per cent (will be restored to 90 per cent by October 1 and 100 per cent by April 1, 2021)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->LOAN FREEZE: RBI Governor Shaktikanta Das has stopped the clock on loan repayments amid an unprecedented three-week lockdown announced by Prime Minister Narendra Modi

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->All lenders can freeze repayments for three months on term loans outstanding March 1

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Lenders allowed to suspend interest payments on working capital facilities for three months; accumulated interest can be paid later and the loans won't be in default

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->The steps add to previous measures which allow a one-off restructuring of loans to small businesses that were in default as of January 1

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Loans to commercial property projects that are delayed for reasons beyond the control of the developer are allowed to be treated as standard for another year

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->REGULATORY DEFERRALS: Implementation of stricter regulations have been delayed

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Rules requiring banks to fund their activities through stable sources has been deferred to October 1 from April 1

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Completion of Capital Conservation Buffer pushed to September 30 from March 31

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Lenders allowed an additional 90 days to reach a resolution plan on large accounts in default (April 17)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->SPECIAL WINDOWS: These include support for corporate borrowers as well as rural industry

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->TLTRO 1.0 -- Rs 1 lakh crore of targeted long term funds from the central bank to banks for investing only in corporate bonds, aimed at easing cash crunch at firms (on April 15, RBI announced new rule capping the exposure of any bank to a single entity at 10 per cent of TLTRO funds invested)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->TLTRO 2.0 -- initial Rs 50,000 crore targeted at shadow lenders, with at least half going to lower rated firms (April 17)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Special refinance to umbrella organizations -- Rs 50,000 crore to go to pan-India financiers like Sidbi, Nabard, NHB that affordably fund the rural sector and agriculture (April 17)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Credit line for mutual funds -- Rs 50,000 crore credit facility running April 27 to May 11, or until the funds are utilized, whichever is earlier (April 27)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->HIGHER PROVISIONS: Banks ordered to maintain higher provision of 10 per cent on all frozen loans spread over the January-March and April-June quarters, which can be adjusted later against actual slippages (April 17)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->DIVIDENDS HALTED: Banks can't pay dividends for the year ended March 31 to conserve capital. Decision will be reviews on the basis of their financial position on September 30

**Sovereign Bonds and Rupee:**

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->MORE MONEY: The RBI has been injecting additional liquidity in the banking system to keep down bond yields

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->At its February policy review, the RBI said it will provide Rs 1 lakh crore of one- and three-year cash at the policy rate via long-term repo operations to help monetary transmission (Feb. 6)

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->The RBI ramped up these measures in March and April

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Two variable rate repo operations of Rs 50,000 crore to fine-tune liquidity at the financial year end

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Enhanced a temporary liquidity tap for primary bond underwriters to Rs 10,000 crore from Rs 2,800 crore

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Rs 1 lakh crore of LTROs

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Open market purchase of govt bonds worth Rs 10,000 crore March 20; another total Rs 30,000 crore of OMO purchases March 24 and March 26

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Rs 1 lakh crore via 16-day variable rate repos

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->INVITING FOREIGNERS: India opened up a wide swath of its sovereign bond market to overseas investors, taking its biggest step yet to secure access to global indexes as the government embarks on a record borrowing plan

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->LIMIT BORROWING: The government announced a fiscal first-half borrowing number that's lower than what traders expected, as it seeks to check any rise in yields amid a global risk aversion that's sparked outflows from emerging markets

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->MORE DOLLARS: RBI pledged to inject dollars through dollar-rupee swaps

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->Two $2 billion swap lines each for March 16 and March 23 provided $2.7 billion

<!--\[if !supportLists]-->·<!--\[endif]-->SHORTER TRADING HOURS: Trading in sovereign debt and the rupee will be held from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. Mumbai time starting April 7 through April 30. These markets normally worked from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m.
